2023 Confirmed Driver Lineups

Red Bull
Max Verstappen Sergio Perez

Due to their impeccable season, Red Bull retains both drivers for the 2023 season.
Ferrari
Charles Leclerc Carlos Sainz

No changes being made for 2023
Mercedes
Lewis Hamilton George Russell

No changes being made for 2023
Alpine
Pierre Gasly Esteban Ocon

Pierre Gasly will replace Fernando Alonso's seat for the 2023 season. Gasly is leaving Alpha Tauri after the 2022 season.
McLaren
Lando Norris Oscar Piastri

Oscar Piastri will replace Daniel Riccardo for the 2023 season. Riccardo has had a rough time driving the McLaren and due to his teammate consistently outscoring him, McLaren decided to bring in Formula 1 rookie, Piastri. Alfa Romeo
Valtteri Bottas Zhou Guanyu

No changes being made for 2023
Haas
Kevin Magnussen

Kevin Magnussen has been confirmed for the 2023 Haas seat, but Mich Schumacher has not been. Due to his lack of points during the 2022 season, Schumacher's 2023 season is yet to be confirmed.
Alpha Tauri
Yuki Tsunoda Nyck De Vries

Nyck De Vries will take Pierre Gasly's seat at Alpha Tauri for the 2023 season. In Gasly's contract, it was stated he could switch teams if a suitable replacement for the team was available. Due to De Vries' point-scoring drive for Williams during the 2022 season, Alpha Tauri saw him as a suitable replacement and allowed Gasly to drive for Alpine.
Aston Martin
Lance Stroll Fernando Alonso

Due to Sebastian Vettel's retirement, Aston Martin was able to pull in world champion driver Fernando Alonso for the 2023 season. Fernando is leaving Alpine after the 2022 season.
Williams
Alexander Albon

Due to lack of points, Williams announced Nicholas Latifi would be leaving the team after the 2022 season. The second Williams seat is still unconfirmed, but it is rumored a Formula 2 driver may move up to Formula 1 to fill this seat.
